Competitive advantages that lead to market dominance, either by a single company or by a small number of essentially equivalent firms, are much more likely to be found when the arena is local—bounded either geographically or in product space—than when it is large and scattered. That is because the sources of competitive advantage, as we will see, tend to be local and specific, not general or diffuse.
